Johan Montesinos Blog

Where ideas in mathematics, physics and computer science meets typography in the pursuit of clear communication.

I am an Electrical Engineer and Project Manager driven by a singular belief: that technology is the deliberate crystallization of human intent into functional form.. My work sits at the intersection of hardware, software, and strategic execution, but my true fascination lies in utilizing innovation as an existential tool for improving people's lives.

This blog represents a confluence of all my passions: the elegance of mathematical, phycis, computer science thought and the precision of typographical design. Inspired by the LaTeX typesetting system, which has been the gold standard for academic publishing since the 1980s, I strive to present complex ideas with clarity and aesthetic refinement.

Our Mission

Purpose

To make mathematics, phycis, computer science, and technical subjects accessible through clear writing and beautiful typography. I believe that good design is not merely decorative—it serves comprehension, helping readers navigate complex ideas with confidence.

Every article published here undergoes careful consideration of both content and form. I write with the conviction that technical subjects deserve the same attention to prose style as any literary work. Mathematical notation receives proper spacing and sizing. Paragraphs flow with deliberate rhythm. Headers provide clear signposts through conceptual terrain.

Core Values

  • Rigor and Accessibility
    Maintain mathematical precision while explaining concepts in language accessible to dedicated learners at various levels.
  • Aesthetic Excellence
    Typography matters. I employ principles from centuries of print tradition to create documents that are pleasant to read on screen.
  • Pedagogical Clarity
    Every article aims to teach, building understanding through careful exposition, concrete examples, and thoughtful progression of ideas.
  • Intellectual Honesty
    I acknowledge limitations, cite sources properly, and distinguish between established results and speculative ideas.

Who I Write For

My audience includes students deepening their understanding of technical subjects, professionals seeking clear explanations of adjacent fields, and curious minds who appreciate the beauty inherent in mathematical structures. Whether you're encountering these topics for the first time or reviewing familiar material from new perspectives, I hope to offer value through careful exposition.

I assume readers bring intellectual curiosity and willingness to engage with challenging ideas. We do not assume extensive prerequisites—necessary background is explained or referenced. Our goal is not to impress with obscurity but to illuminate with clarity.

The LaTeX Influence

Donald Knuth created TeX in the late 1970s because he found existing typesetting systems inadequate for his mathematical texts. Leslie Lamport later developed LaTeX as a more accessible interface to TeX's power. These tools transformed academic publishing by giving authors control over typographical quality previously available only to professional typesetters.

This blog embraces LaTeX's philosophy: content and presentation both matter. I use Computer Modern fonts, designed by Knuth specifically for mathematical typesetting. We employ careful spacing, proper paragraph indentation, and thoughtful use of whitespace. These choices reflect our belief that how ideas are presented influences how they are understood.

Looking Forward

This project continues to evolve. I welcome dialogue with readers, suggestions for topics, and constructive feedback on both content and presentation. Mathematics and typography are both living disciplines, and we aim to grow alongside them while maintaining the timeless principles that make technical communication effective.

Thank you for reading. I hope you find something here that sparks insight, satisfies curiosity, or simply provides a moment of appreciation for the elegant structures underlying our technical disciplines.
